Number Sense

Advanced Understanding of Quantities and Concepts

Place Value and Magnitude of Numbers:
Scientific notation expresses very large or very small numbers using powers of 10. Example: The speed of light is approximately (3 × 10^8) meters per second. It simplifies calculations and represents precision.

Significant Figures:
Significant figures indicate the precision of a measurement. Rules for determining significant figures: Non-zero digits are always significant. Leading zeros (zeros before any non-zero digit) are not significant. Trailing zeros (zeros after non-zero digits) in a decimal number are significant. Trailing zeros in a whole number without a decimal point may or may not be significant (context matters).

Modular Arithmetic (Congruences), Complex Numbers, and more.

Prime Numbers, Factors, and Divisibility Rules at an Advanced Level

Prime Factorization:
Every positive integer can be expressed as a product of prime numbers. Example: Prime factorization of 60: (60 = 2^2 × 3 × 5).

Mersenne Primes:
Mersenne primes are of the form (2^n - 1), where (n) is a prime. Example: (2^3 - 1 = 7) is a Mersenne prime.

Fermat’s Little Theorem, and more.

In-Depth Topics: Number Theory, Exponents, and Logarithms

Diophantine Equations:
Diophantine equations involve finding integer solutions to polynomial equations. Example: Fermat’s Last Theorem: (x^n + y^n = z^n) has no non-zero integer solutions for (n > 2).
